Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine ground beef, egg, breadcrumbs, Parmesan cheese, minced garlic, chopped parsley, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  2. Form the mixture into golf ball-sized meatballs.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Cook the meatballs until browned on all sides, about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Add marinara sauce to the skillet with the meatballs. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until meatballs are cooked through.
  5. Meanwhile, cook spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water according to package instructions until al dente.
  6. Drain the spaghetti and return it to the pot.
  7. Add the meatballs and sauce to the spaghetti. Toss gently to combine.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with additional Parmesan cheese and chopped parsley if desired.

Nutrition Information (per serving):

Calories: 650 | Protein: 35g | Carbohydrates: 80g | Fat: 25g | Fiber: 5g

Chef's Tips:

  • For juicier meatballs, use a mixture of ground beef and ground pork.
  • Don't overmix the meatball mixture to keep them tender.
  • For a healthier option, try whole wheat spaghetti or zucchini noodles.
